High-Performance Structural Alloys
Research Group

We design next-generation structural alloys through computational alloy design, additive manufacturing, laser cladding, and advanced microstructure engineering. Our research integrates thermodynamics, processing, characterization, and performance to engineer alloys for demanding service environments.

RESEARCH

Research Areas

Additive Manufacturing

Alloy development and processing strategies for laser powder bed fusion and directed energy deposition.

Multi-Principal Element Alloys

Computationally guided design of Fe-based complex concentrated alloys with tailored microstructures and properties.

Surface Engineering

Laser cladding, wear-resistant coatings, corrosion protection, and high-temperature applications.

OUR APPROACH

How We Develop Structural Alloys

Computational Design

We employ CALPHAD-based thermodynamic calculations, computational alloy design, and ICME concepts to accelerate the development of advanced structural alloys.

Materials Processing

Novel alloys are fabricated using additive manufacturing, laser cladding, casting, and heat-treatment routes to achieve controlled microstructures.

Advanced Characterization

Microstructure evolution is investigated using optical microscopy, SEM, EBSD, XRD, and mechanical testing to establish processing–structure relationships.

Performance Evaluation

Mechanical, tribological, and corrosion performance are evaluated to engineer alloys for demanding industrial and high-temperature applications.
